Introduction

Disc Golf is a hobby in which players throw a disc at a target in the fewest amount of attempts possible. The rules are closely related to golf. Courses are most often free and can be found in public parks, and are typically 9 or 18 holes long. However, there are some private pay to play courses. A "hole" is completed by throwing a disc from a tee pad (or designated area) toward a target, referred to as a basket (Figure 1). Subsequent throws are from where the disc lands on the course. A hole is completed when the disc comes to rest in the basket, whether it is caught by the chains or not. The goal is to complete the hole in the fewest number of shots.

Common Terms for Scores on a Hole

  • Par means shooting the predetermined average score
  • Ace is a hole in one regardless of par
  • Albatross is three under the hole's par
  • Eagle is two under par
  • Birdie is one under
  • Bogey is one over
  • Double Bogey is two over
  • Triple Bogey is three over
  • Quadruple Bogey is four over
  • Anything above quadruple bogey is referred to as "+X"

Leagues and Tournaments

Leagues are weekly rounds open to all players, entry fees are often between $10 and $15. There are payouts for the top players and best shots such as aces (hole in one). Figure 8 is a table of the parks in Charlotte that currently have weekly leagues. If you would like to find out more about the Charlotte disc golf leagues click this link.

Tournaments are one time competitions. They are either sanctioned by the Professional Disc Golf Association (PDGA) or unsanctioned. Amongst sanctioned tournaments there are 3 tiers C, B and A. C is the lowest and A is the highest. Competitors often play two rounds in one day on the weekend. The Professional Tour

The Disc Golf Pro Tour is the pinnacle of competition in professional disc golf. It features men's and women's professional divisions. The DGPT championship is played here in Charlotte, first place in each division comes with a $30,000 payout.

In addition to the DGPT championship there is also the "United States Disc Golf Championship" in Rock Hill, South Carolina. It is played on the grounds of Winthrop University.
