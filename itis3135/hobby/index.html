Disc Golf

Introduction

Disc Golf is a hobby in which players throw a disc at a target in the fewest amount of attempts possible. The rules are closely related to golf. Courses are most often free and can be found in public parks, and are typically 9 or 18 holes long. However, there are some private pay to play courses. A "hole" is completed by throwing a disc from a tee pad (or designated area) toward a target, referred to as a basket (Figure 1). Subsequent throws are from where the disc lands on the course. A hole is completed when the disc comes to rest in the basket, whether it is caught by the chains or not. The goal is to complete the hole in the fewest number of shots.

The Common Terms for Scores on a Hole

  • A Par means shooting the predetermined average score
  • An Ace is a hole in one regardless of par
  • An Albatross is three under the hole's par
  • An Eagle is two under par
  • A Birdie is one under
  • A Bogey is one over
  • A Double Bogey is two over
  • A Triple Bogey is three over
  • A Quadruple Bogey is four over
  • Anything above quadruple bogey is referred to as "+X"

Types of Discs

The majority of discs are made out of polypropene plastic and weigh around 170 grams.

Discs are generally categorized into 3 different types:

  • Driver
  • Midrage
  • Putter
